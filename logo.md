# Logo Brief — "Finances"

A complete brief for creating the app logo. Paste any section into an AI logo generator, or hand it to a designer.

---

## 1. The product in one line

**Finances** — a private, offline-first personal-money app for India. You track spending and budgets, and also grow and see your *whole* financial life: investments, gold, FDs, loans, lending (udhaar), and net worth. Nothing leaves the phone — no accounts, no cloud, no ads.

## 2. Personality / adjectives

Calm · trustworthy · premium · editorial · grown-up · quietly confident.
**Not:** flashy, techy-neon, cartoonish, corporate-bank, crypto-hype.

Think a well-designed print magazine or a fountain-pen ledger — not a fintech startup dashboard. It should feel like something you'd trust with your money and enjoy opening daily.

## 3. What the logo should convey

- **Money / rupee** (Indian, ₹) — the single most important symbol.
- **Personal & private** — it's *your* money, kept safe on your device.
- **Growth / clarity** — a subtle sense of things trending up and being in order.
- Timeless over trendy — should still look good in 5 years.

## 4. Current mark (starting point — feel free to evolve)

A **rust-orange ₹ (rupee) glyph** set inside a **rounded-square "ink" tile** (near-black, ~26px corner radius), with a soft drop shadow — like a premium app icon. The ₹ is drawn in a **serif** style (Instrument Serif), which gives it an editorial, literary feel rather than a generic sans ₹.

This works well and can stay the core idea. Good directions to explore around it:
- ₹ where a stroke subtly doubles as a **rising line/graph** or an upward tick.
- ₹ integrated with a **thin horizontal rule** (nod to the app's hairline "editorial rule" dividers).
- A monogram lockup: the ₹ tile + the wordmark "Finances" in serif italic.

## 5. Exact brand palette (use these hex values)

| Role | Hex | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|
| Paper (background) | `#F5F0E4` | warm off-white, the app's canvas |
| Ink (primary dark) | `#1B1710` | near-black, warm brown-black — used for the icon tile |
| Rust / brand accent | `#D9481C` | the hero color — the ₹ is this color |
| Green (positive) | `#4E9E6A` | gains / "to collect" |
| On-dark green | `#84C79B` | green on the ink tile |
| On-dark rust | `#F0844F` | rust on the ink tile |
| Gold accent (optional) | `#C9972E` | warm metallic, for a premium touch |

**Primary combo:** rust `#D9481C` ₹ on ink `#1B1710` tile. Alternative: ink ₹ on paper `#F5F0E4`.
Keep it to **2–3 colors max.** No gradients-as-crutch, but a *very* subtle warm gradient on the tile is acceptable.

## 6. Typography

- Display / wordmark: **Instrument Serif** (elegant, high-contrast serif; italic version has real character). The word "Finances" is normally shown in **serif italic**.
- Body / supporting: **DM Sans** (clean geometric sans).
- If the logo includes text, prefer the serif for "Finances" and reserve the sans for any tagline.

Optional tagline: *"Your money, fully offline."*

## 7. Formats needed

- **App icon** — square, works at 48px up to 512px; the ₹-on-tile mark, no text, centered with padding. Also a maskable/safe-area version (keep the glyph within the center 80%).
- **Horizontal lockup** — mark + "Finances" wordmark, for README/website headers.
- **Monochrome** — single-color ink version (for stamps, light backgrounds) and a reversed paper-on-ink version.
- **Favicon** — must read clearly at 16px (the ₹ alone).

## 8. Do / Don't

**Do**
- Keep it simple, geometric, and legible at tiny sizes.
- Make the ₹ unmistakable and the focal point.
- Use the warm, editorial palette above.
- Aim for flat / minimal with at most a soft shadow.

**Don't**
- No coins, piggy banks, wallets, dollar signs, or clip-art money.
- No neon, no blue "fintech" gradients, no 3D glass, no drop-shadow overload.
- No busy backgrounds or more than 3 colors.
- Don't make it look like a bank or a crypto exchange.
